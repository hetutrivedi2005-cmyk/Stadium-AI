Crowd intelligence,

Every venue is modelled as a graph of ~2,400 nodes — gates, concourses, vomitories, seat blocks, restrooms, egress paths. StadiumAI runs a continuous simulation against the live sensor feed.

How it works

Four layers.
One coordinated response.

Step 01

Ingest

150+ pre-built connectors span turnstiles, CCTV analytics, transit APIs, ticketing, IoT and weather — deployed in under two weeks.

Step 02

Model

A digital twin of your venue is calibrated against historical match data and rehearsed against simulated incidents before kick-off.

Step 03

Decide

Playbooks combine ML forecasts with your operations SOPs — surfacing what to do next, not just what's happening now.

Step 04

Act

Push actions to signage, PA, staff radios and the fan app — with a full audit trail for post-match review and regulatory sign-off.

A standard deployment is 6–8 weeks: 2 weeks of connector integration, 2 weeks of digital-twin calibration, and 2–4 weeks of rehearsal — including full-capacity dry runs before your first ticketed event.

StadiumAI replaces fragmented telemetry stacks, vendor dashboard silos, and static spreadsheets. It integrates with existing hardware (turnstiles, CCTV, and building management systems) to supply a single dynamic operating system.

Data processing is hosted securely on-premise at the stadium or inside a local sovereign cloud instance compliance-certified by host-country requirements. Processing pipelines strictly discard PII at source.

The accessibility layer processes live infrastructure feeds to isolate staircases, lifts, and escalators. It exports low-stress, ramp-friendly routes directly to staff terminals and the visitor navigation companion apps.

StadiumAI operates an edge-first offline-tolerant fallback architecture. If external cloud access drops, localized edge servers situated inside the venue manage active loops autonomously without data loss.
